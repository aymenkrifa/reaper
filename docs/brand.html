reaper

a tidy harvest of listening ports

01 · the idea

Before the reaper was a spooky figure, it was a farmhand. Reaping is harvest work — and in Unix it is literally the technical term: when a process finishes, the kernel reaps it. Reaping is janitorial, not sinister. This identity takes the name at its word: your ports are a field, running processes are the crop, and reaper is the tidy little tool that brings the harvest in.

No skulls, no hoods, no graves. Harvest gold, field green, warm paper — with a dusk mode in reserve for terminals and GitHub dark, because the crop colors work under either sky.

02 · the mark

A wheat ear built from one repeating grain shape — a lens set at 33° — with three awns, a leaf, and a green stem. The signature detail: one grain is always detached, drifting down-right. That grain is the reaped process. Serene, not violent; the ear stands, the harvest happens anyway.

construction — grain angle, spacing, clear space. Scale the master asset; never redraw the ear.

03 · lockups

The wordmark is Bricolage Grotesque (weight 760) — sturdy, warm, a little hand-hewn, like a good tool handle. Lowercase always, ending in a single tilted grain instead of a period: the sentence ends, the grain falls. In lockups the ear keeps all its grains — the detached grain lives only in the standalone mark. All wordmark glyphs are outlined in the SVG assets, so they render identically everywhere, GitHub READMEs included.

dusk lockup — flour text, stem green lifted to #5FA36B. Default for dark UIs and GitHub dark mode.
daylight lockup — soil ink on warm paper, for docs and print.

04 · colors

A field palette with two skies. Daylight (warm paper) for documents and print; dusk (deep loam brown, never neutral black) for terminals and GitHub dark mode. The crop colors never change between them — only the sky does. And one poppy: real wheat fields grow a few, this identity is allowed exactly one. Poppy red appears only on the reap confirmation. Anywhere else, it's a weed.

05 · typography

Three faces, three jobs. Bricolage Grotesque speaks only in the wordmark and headlines. Inter does the reading — documentation, UI chrome, this paragraph. JetBrains Mono does the running — code, the terminal, labels, anything a machine touches.

good ports make good neighbors
text · Inter 400–600 · documentation and interface copy
Inter carries everything meant for people: guides, release notes, settings labels. Plain, legible, no personality games — the personality lives in the mark and the headlines, not in the reading.
code · JetBrains Mono 400–700 · the terminal and everything machine-adjacent

06 · voice

Calm farmhand: plain verbs, short sentences, quiet satisfaction in tidiness. reaper never "kills" anything in its copy — it reaps. The word is older, kinder, and more accurate to what the OS actually does.

07 · rules & files

crop colors on either sky
don't change the crop — no other hues
don't over-ripen — no neon saturation
no storm damage — the ear stands upright
no stretching — grains keep their 33°
no busy surfaces — the field needs sky

And one more: in the standalone mark the detached grain stays detached. Re-attaching it deletes the story. In lockups the ear is whole — the falling grain became the punctuation.
